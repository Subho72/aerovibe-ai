1. Overview of the Closed-Loop System

Edge AI AeroVibe is designed to monitor turbine blade stress signals and initiate automatic mechanical isolation within milliseconds of structural crack detection. This local containment process functions autonomously with tri-layer safety filters (Filter A: Sensor Fusion, Filter B: Temporal Voting, Filter C: Human Veto) to prevent false-alarm shutdowns during commercial flight operations.

2. Steps to Run the Flight Deck Test

  1. Verify Normal Operation: Navigate to the Flight Deck page. The casing sensor graph (oscilloscope) shows a low-amplitude blue sinusoidal rotation wave, and the waterfall spectrogram displays low-frequency harmonics. The CNN status is green and reads HEALTHY (99.8%).
  2. Flip Switch Cover: Click the dark red button labeled GUARD CLOSED. It rotates up in 3D space, changing to GUARD Flipped, and unlocks the inject options.
  3. Inject Severe Strike: Click the glowing red INJECT SEVERE STRIKE button.
    • A warning banner flashes: AUTOMATED ISOLATION IN 3.00s.
    • The [VETO AUTOMATED SHUTDOWN] button arms. You have 3 seconds to click it to cancel the safety trip.
    • If the timer reaches 0, the engine shuts down in 17.2ms. The Fuel Solenoid locks to SHUT, Variable Guide Vanes swivels to stall pitch, and the emergency alert overlay displays.
  4. Inject Soft Impact: Click the glowing orange INJECT SOFT IMPACT button.
    • The engine remains active but registers a warning. RUL drops to 35 minutes and Wear index increases to 42%.
    • A dotted orange Alternative Diversion Route displays on the world map routing the plane to Mumbai (BOM) Hub.
    • A 30-to-45-Minute Precautionary Alert warning banner displays.
  5. Reset Loop: Click the RESET SAFETY LOOP button to re-arm.

3. FADEC Co-Processor & Avionics Bus Integration

Edge AI AeroVibe integrates directly as an Electronic Engine Control (EEC) card within the FADEC housing. Critical shutoffs (solenoids and vanes) use hardwired analog relays to execute in under 2ms, bypassing software stacks. Status reporting is packed into ARINC 429 digital words (e.g. Label 340 for Safety State, Label 345 for Wear) and transmitted to the flight deck display systems over standard AFDX networks. This isolates the local 17.2ms emergency safety loop from the cockpit display sync lag (3.14s).

4. Acoustic Casing Calibration Diagnostics

Different turbofan models (e.g., GEnx vs. CFM LEAP) present different background resonance and vibration baselines. Upon deployment, technicians execute the calibration command to run ground sweep tests, normalizing baseline background noise and uploading target frequency masks. Piezoelectric acoustic sensors capture high-frequency elastic stress waves ($20\text{ kHz}$ to $100\text{ kHz}$) caused by micro-fractures, filtering out low-frequency blade rotation hum.

⚠️ Critical Operational Safety Note

Once a blade crack is identified and confirmed by the tri-layer filter system, fuel supply is isolated immediately to prevent combustion forces from driving a shedding blade through the engine casing. Veto manual override is bypassed if Filter A and Filter B detect extreme mechanical failure bounds.
